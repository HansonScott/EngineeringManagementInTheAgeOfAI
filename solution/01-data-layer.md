> *This document is in progress. It will cover: the structure and purpose of each data file, the init skill that loads them, and what the experience of using Claude looks and feels like once the data layer is in place.*

**What this covers:** Preferences (working style, communication expectations), Contacts (team, peers, adjacent people with handles and context), Links (tools and spaces navigated repeatedly), Patterns (tool-specific conventions and known quirks), Memory (cross-session persistence), and the Init skill that ties them together.

**The key insight:** Building a data layer isn't a one-time project — it's a practice. You add to it whenever something requires re-explanation. Over time, the overhead of context-setting drops toward zero and the quality of every interaction improves.
