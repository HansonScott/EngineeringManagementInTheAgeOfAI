> *This essay is in progress. It will cover: the moment the directive landed, why the IC path is obvious and the EM path isn't, and what that gap reveals about the nature of management work.*

**Core argument:** When AI became a baseline expectation for engineering excellence, individual contributors had a clear path — write faster, review smarter, debug with a partner. Engineering managers faced a harder question: most of what we do isn't code. It's judgment, relationships, and information synthesis. The inflection point isn't just about tools. It's about what the role is actually for.
