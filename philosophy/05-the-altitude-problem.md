# The Altitude Problem

*[← The Objectivity Problem](./04-the-objectivity-problem.md) | [Home](../README.md)*

---

> *This essay is in progress. It will cover: what "operating at the right altitude" means for an EM, the gravitational pull of tactical work, and how to build systems that surface altitude drift before it becomes a pattern.*

**Core argument:** The EM role is strategic by definition — your leverage is through your team, not through your own output. But tactical work is immediate, concrete, and satisfying in ways that strategic work isn't. Without a system that actively monitors altitude, most managers drift downward over time, doing more IC work and less of the coaching, direction-setting, and gap-closing that the role actually requires. The question isn't whether you'll be pulled tactical — you will — it's whether you'll notice it.
