---

> *This piece is in progress. It will cover: how ad-hoc use revealed patterns, how patterns became custom skills, and the pivotal moment when efficiency stopped being the point.*

**Core arc:** Once the data layer was in place, the natural next move was capability — connecting to the tools already in use (Slack, Jira, Zoom, documents) and encoding the patterns that kept repeating. Coming back from vacation always involved the same re-orientation. Every 1:1 involved the same preparation. Every week involved the same status updates. Encoding those patterns as skills wasn't a technological leap — it was just noticing what repeated and making it repeatable on purpose.

The Moneyball moment came later: realizing that doing the same things faster was only valuable if those things were the right things. Efficiency had been the question. Impact replaced it.
